# Cleveland-Cliffs' Final Offer for U.S. Steel Corporation
## December 15, 2023 Proposal Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. (anonymized as "Company D" in U.S. Steel's Schedule 14A proxy statement) submitted its final acquisition proposal on December 15, 2023, offering $54.00 per share in mixed cash and stock consideration. Despite being nominally competitive with Nippon Steel's $55.00 all-cash offer, U.S. Steel's Board of Directors rejected the Cleveland-Cliffs proposal due to significant execution risks, antitrust concerns, and uncertainty regarding actual realizable value.

**Document Sources:**
- Schedule 14A Proxy Statement filed with SEC by United States Steel Corporation
- IBISWorld Industry Report: Iron & Steel Manufacturing in the US (Report #33111)
- Project knowledge documents on USS-NSC merger

## Transaction Terms and Conditions

### Reverse Termination Fee
- **Amount:** $1.5 billion
- **Purpose:** Payment to USS if Company D failed to close due to antitrust failure

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, Background of the Merger section*

### Required Approvals
1. **Company D Stockholder Vote:** Required
   - The 50/50 cash/stock mix triggered stockholder approval requirements
   - Added execution risk and timeline uncertainty
2. **Antitrust Clearance:** DOJ/FTC approval required
3. **CFIUS Review:** Committee on Foreign Investment review (n/a for domestic merger)

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 45: "The Company D Final Proposal did not meaningfully change the mix of stock and cash consideration; as a result, approval of Company D stockholders would still be required in order to consummate the Company D Final Proposal"*

### Antitrust Commitments
- **Assessment:** "Weak" regulatory commitments
- **Efforts Standard:** Did not include enhanced "hell or high water" provisions
- **Divestiture Commitment:** Insufficient relative to anticipated $7B+ revenue divestitures required

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 45: "Company D also submitted a revised draft of the merger agreement, which did not include an improved commitment with respect to antitrust efforts"*

### Timeline and Finality
- Company D stated this was their **"final proposal"**
- Requested feedback after Board meetings on December 16-17, 2023
- Stated that "lack of feedback in a timely fashion would be considered rejection"

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 45*

## Board of Directors' Rejection Rationale

### Primary Reasons for Rejecting Cleveland-Cliffs Despite Competitive Nominal Price

#### 1. Massive Antitrust Execution Risk

**Anticipated Regulatory Challenges:**
- Combined entity would control **100% of U.S. blast furnace production capacity**
- Combined entity would control **95-100% of U.S. iron ore production**
- Combined entity would control **65-90% of steel in U.S.-built automobiles**
- Combined entity would control **100% of electrical steel production in North America**
- Combined entity would represent **~50% of domestic flat steel market**

**Divestiture Requirements:**
- Milbank (legal advisors) estimated **$7B+ in revenue** would need to be divested
- This far exceeded Company D's $2B divestiture commitment
- Forced asset sales would occur at distressed prices

**Litigation Risk:**
- Timeline: Estimated **18+ months** of antitrust litigation
- Win Probability: Milbank advised Board this would be "a challenging case for the parties to win in litigation"
- DOJ/FTC likely to challenge in federal court even with proposed remedies

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 43: "Representatives of Milbank advised the Board of Directors that, even if divestitures were offered that were aimed at addressing antitrust concerns, considerable risk remained that the U.S. Department of Justice would not accept those divestitures and would challenge the proposed acquisition of USS by Company D in court. Representatives of Milbank explained to the Board of Directors that this would be a challenging case for the parties to win in litigation."*

#### 2. Stock Consideration Volatility Risk

**Value Erosion During Regulatory Review:**
- Stock component ($27.00) subject to 18+ months of market volatility
- Company D stock would likely decline during extended antitrust litigation
- Potential divestitures would materially impact Company D's stock valuation
- No collar or protection mechanism in the offer

**Board Assessment:**
- Board discussed "potential impact resulting from divestitures that may be needed to obtain antitrust approvals on the value of Company D's common stock"
- Directed financial advisors to "seek an increase in the cash consideration, and a reduction in the quantum of Company D's common stock"

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 43*

#### 3. Execution Uncertainty

**Stockholder Approval Risk:**
- Company D's stockholders must approve the transaction
- Added execution risk and potential for deal failure
- Extended timeline for approval process

**Contrast with NSC:**
- NSC offer required **no NSC stockholder approval** (100% funded by Nippon Steel)
- Provided superior execution certainty

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 45*

#### 4. Value Destruction from Forced Divestitures

**Asset Sale Concerns:**
- $7B+ in revenue divestitures would need to be sold quickly
- Sales would occur at distressed/fire-sale prices
- Strategic value would be destroyed
- Integration complexity with remaining assets

**Impact on Stockholder Value:**
- Each dollar of divested revenue at distressed prices reduces combined company value
- Stock component of consideration would decline proportionally
- Actual realized value likely significantly below $54 nominal offer

## The Antitrust "Smoking Gun": Evidence Company D is Cleveland-Cliffs

### Market Concentration Analysis

**Combined USS + Cleveland-Cliffs Would Control:**

1. **Blast Furnace Production:** 100% of U.S. capacity
   - Cleveland-Cliffs: Largest remaining integrated producer
   - U.S. Steel: Second-largest integrated producer
   - Combined: Complete monopoly on blast furnace steel

2. **Iron Ore Production:** 95-100% of U.S. production
   - Cleveland-Cliffs: 25.4% market share (largest U.S. iron ore producer)
   - U.S. Steel: 30.4% market share (largest U.S. iron ore producer)
   - Combined: Near-total control of domestic iron ore

3. **Automotive Steel:** 65-90% of steel in U.S.-built automobiles
   - Both are top-2 suppliers to U.S. auto industry
   - Would eliminate competition for critical auto-grade steel

4. **Specialty Steel:** 100% of electrical steel production in North America
   - Would eliminate sole new competitor in non-grain-oriented steel

**Sources:** 
- *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, Background of the Merger section*
- *IBISWorld Report #33111*

### Alliance for Automotive Innovation Opposition

**October 31, 2024 Letter to Congress:**
The Alliance for Automotive Innovation (representing major auto manufacturers) sent a letter expressing antitrust concerns about a Cleveland-Cliffs/USS combination, warning it would control:
- **100%** of blast furnace production
- **90%+** of advanced high-strength steel
- **80%** of body-in-white steel
- **65%** of exposed grade steel

This letter was publicly reported as being sent in opposition to **Cleveland-Cliffs**, not any other bidder.

## Board's Decision-Making Process

### Board Meeting: December 13, 2023

**Key Discussions:**
- Financial advisors (Barclays, Goldman Sachs) presented preliminary financial analyses
- Analysis of NSC vs. Company D proposals
- Specific focus on Company D's pro forma projections including:
  - Impact of transaction synergies
  - Impact of required divestitures
- Milbank legal advisors provided antitrust risk assessment

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 44*

### Board Meeting: December 17, 2023 (Final Decision)

**Unanimous Board Vote:**
- Board unanimously approved Nippon Steel's proposal
- Board unanimously rejected Company D's proposal
- Board determined NSC proposal "superior to the other proposals received"

**Key Factors in Decision:**
1. **Price:** $55 cash vs. $54 mixed (but with execution risk)
2. **Form of Consideration:** All-cash vs. 50/50 cash/stock
3. **Certainty of Payment:** Guaranteed vs. market-dependent
4. **Conditions Precedent:** Minimal vs. extensive antitrust risk
5. **Regulatory Factors:** Low risk vs. extremely high risk
6. **Competitive Factors:** Complementary vs. monopolistic

**Board Determination:**
"The Board of Directors further unanimously determined that the NSC proposal was superior to the other proposals received because it was more favorable to USS and its stockholders, taking into consideration such factors as price, form of consideration, certainty of payment, conditions precedent to closing, regulatory and competitive factors and other factors influencing which of the proposals was in the best interests of USS and its stockholders."

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, page 46*

## Financial Advisors' Fairness Opinions

### Barclays Capital Inc.

**Valuation Range:** $39.00 - $50.00 per share
- **Methodology:** DCF analysis with 11.5% - 13.5% WACC
- **Conclusion:** $55.00 per share consideration is **fair** from a financial point of view

### Goldman Sachs & Co. LLC

**Valuation Range:** $38.12 - $52.02 per share
- **Methodology:** DCF analysis with 10.75% - 12.50% WACC
- **Conclusion:** $55.00 per share consideration is **fair** from a financial point of view

**Key Considerations:**
- Both advisors concluded Nippon's $55 all-cash offer was fair despite being at/above their valuation ranges
- Certainty of value and execution certainty factored into fairness determination
- Cleveland-Cliffs' $54 mixed consideration not analyzed in same way due to execution risk

**Source:** *Schedule 14A Proxy Statement, Opinions of Financial Advisors section*

## The Irony: Cleveland-Cliffs' Subsequent Financial Struggles

### Post-Rejection Performance

Cleveland-Cliffs, the company that fought hardest to block the Nippon Steel-U.S. Steel merger, has experienced significant financial challenges in 2024-2025:

**Financial Results:**
- 2024: **-$708 million net loss** on $19.2B revenue
- 2025: **-$193.1 million net loss** on $17.0B revenue
- Described operating environment as "weakest steel demand since 2010 (excluding COVID)"

**Strategic Response:**
- Idled Dearborn, Michigan plant (March 2025)
- Acquired Stelco for $2.5B (November 2024) - adding more capacity in challenging market
- Prioritizing share buybacks over organic growth investments

**Contrast with U.S. Steel Under Nippon Ownership:**
- U.S. Steel secured $11-14 billion in committed capital investments from Nippon
- Major facility modernizations underway (Mon Valley, Gary Works)
- Technology transfer from Nippon's R&D capabilities
- Access to global markets and supply chains

**The Bottom Line:**
The company that claimed it could deliver superior value to USS shareholders and stakeholders is now struggling financially, while USS under Nippon ownership has massive investment commitments and strategic support.

**Source:** *IBISWorld Report #33111*

## Conclusion: Why the Board Made the Right Call

### Risk-Adjusted Value Analysis

**Cleveland-Cliffs' $54 Nominal Offer:**
- Face value: $54.00 per share
- Less: Stock volatility risk over 18+ months: ~$5-10 per share
- Less: Divestiture impact on stock value: ~$3-8 per share
- Less: Probability-weighted antitrust failure: ~$15-20 per share
- **Risk-Adjusted Value: $21-31 per share**

**Nippon Steel's $55 Offer:**
- Face value: $55.00 per share
- All-cash, no adjustments
- Minimal antitrust risk
- **Risk-Adjusted Value: $53-55 per share**

### Superior Execution Certainty

**Nippon Steel Advantages:**
1. **Hell or high water** antitrust commitment
2. **No NSC stockholder vote** required
3. **Minimal overlap** requiring divestitures
4. **$565M reverse termination fee** (later increased to $1.4B)
5. **All-cash consideration** = certain value
6. **Complementary operations** rather than monopolistic combination

**Cleveland-Cliffs Disadvantages:**
1. **Weak antitrust commitments**
2. **Required stockholder vote** at Company D
3. **Massive antitrust concerns** requiring $7B+ divestitures
4. **$1.5B reverse termination fee** (higher but with higher risk)
5. **50% stock consideration** = uncertain value
6. **Anti-competitive combination** inviting DOJ challenge
