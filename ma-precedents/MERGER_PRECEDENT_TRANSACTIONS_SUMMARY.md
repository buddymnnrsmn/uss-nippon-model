## Transaction 1: Cleveland-Cliffs Acquisition of AK Steel (2020)

### Basic Deal Information
- **Acquirer:** Cleveland-Cliffs Inc.
- **Target:** AK Steel Holding Corporation
- **Deal Value:** $1.1 billion equity value; $3.0 billion enterprise value
- **Announcement Date:** December 2, 2019
- **Completion Date:** March 13, 2020

### Financial Metrics
- **Purchase Multiple:** 5.6x LTM Adjusted EBITDA
- **Target 2019 EBITDA:** Approximately $536 million (implied from EV/EBITDA multiple)
- **Target 2019 Adjusted EBITDA Guidance:** $450-465 million (company guidance)
- **Premium:** 16% based on closing prices; 27% based on 30-day VWAP

### Deal Structure
- **Type:** All-stock transaction (stock-for-stock merger)
- **Exchange Ratio:** 0.400 Cliffs shares for each AK Steel share
- **Implied Price per AK Steel Share:** $3.36
- **Post-Merger Ownership:** Cliffs shareholders 68%; AK Steel shareholders 32%

### Regulatory Approval Process
- **Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Filing:** Filed in December 2019
- **Early Termination Granted:** January 23, 2020 (FTC)
- **Canadian Competition Clearance:** Received
- **Mexican Competition Commission Clearance:** February 21, 2020 (final approval)
- **Review Period:** ~2.5 months from announcement to final regulatory approval
- **Conditions:** No divestitures or behavioral remedies publicly disclosed

### Strategic Rationale
Vertical integration to create a vertically integrated producer of both flat-rolled carbon steel and iron ore pellets. The combination aimed to enhance competitiveness through operational synergies, reduced raw material costs, and improved market position in the automotive and infrastructure sectors.

## Transaction 2: Cleveland-Cliffs Acquisition of ArcelorMittal USA (2020)

### Basic Deal Information
- **Acquirer:** Cleveland-Cliffs Inc.
- **Target:** ArcelorMittal USA LLC (substantially all operations)
- **Deal Value:** $1.4 billion equity value; ~$3.3 billion enterprise value (including debt)
- **Announcement Date:** September 28, 2020
- **Completion Date:** December 9, 2020

### Financial Metrics
- **Purchase Multiple:** ~6.0x through-the-cycle EBITDA (2017-2019 average)
- **Target Average Annual Revenue (2018-2019):** ~$10.4 billion
- **Target Average Annual Adjusted EBITDA (2018-2019):** ~$700 million
- **Through-Cycle EBITDA Basis:** Average annual EBITDA from 2017-2019

### Deal Structure
- **Type:** Mixed consideration
- **Cash Component:** $505 million (approximately one-third)
- **Stock Component:** 78.2 million shares of Cleveland-Cliffs common stock
- **Preferred Stock:** Non-voting preferred stock valued at ~$373 million
- **Total Consideration Breakdown:**
  - Cash: $505 million
  - Common Stock: Value at announcement
  - Preferred Stock: $373 million

### Regulatory Approval Process
- **Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Filing:** Filed September/October 2020
- **Early Termination Granted:** November 25, 2020 (FTC Bureau of Competition)
- **Review Period:** ~2 months from announcement to clearance
- **Conditions:** No divestitures or behavioral remedies publicly disclosed
- **Significance:** Regulators determined the combination would not substantially lessen competition

### Strategic Rationale
Transformational acquisition to create the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America with combined annual shipments of ~17 million net tons (2019 basis). The deal provided:
- Scale advantages and market leadership
- 6 steelmaking facilities, 8 finishing facilities
- 2 iron ore mining and pelletizing operations
- 3 coal and cokemaking operations
- Vertical integration from raw materials to finished steel
- Enhanced ability to serve automotive, infrastructure, and manufacturing markets

## Transaction 3: U.S. Steel Acquisition of Big River Steel (2019-2021)

### Basic Deal Information
- **Acquirer:** United States Steel Corporation
- **Target:** Big River Steel Holdings LLC
- **Total Deal Value:** ~$1.474 billion (two-stage acquisition)
- **Initial Investment (49.9%):** November 2019 - $700 million
- **Final Acquisition (50.1%):** December 15, 2020 (announced); January 15, 2021 (closed)
- **Final Tranche Value:** $773-774 million

### Financial Metrics
- **Total Enterprise Value:** ~$1.474 billion
- **Target Valuation (October 2019):** $1,403 million (implied)
- **Post-Acquisition EBITDA Margin:** 32% (Q1 2021)
- **Capacity Addition:** 3.3 million tons annual hot-rolled steel capacity (after Phase II-A expansion)
- **Target Debt (Dec 31, 2020):** ~$1,848 million total debt; ~$1,738 million net debt

### Deal Structure
- **Type:** Two-stage acquisition
- **Stage 1 (2019):** Call option to acquire 49.9% stake for $700 million cash
- **Stage 2 (2020-2021):** Exercise of call option to acquire remaining 50.1% for $774 million
- **Payment Method:** All-cash transactions from balance sheet
- **Total Cash Consideration:** ~$1.474 billion
- **Advisors:**
  - M&A Financial Advisor: Barclays
  - Financing Advisors: PJT Partners, Rothschild & Co.
  - Legal Counsel: Milbank LLP

### Regulatory Approval Process
- **HSR Filing:** Filed in December 2020
- **Regulatory Status:** No public disclosure of extended review or challenges
- **Review Period:** Standard HSR waiting period
- **Conditions:** No divestitures or behavioral remedies publicly disclosed
- **Close Date:** January 15, 2021

### Strategic Rationale
Creation of North America's first "Best of Both" integrated and mini-mill steel company:
- Access to advanced EAF (Electric Arc Furnace) technology with superior efficiency
- State-of-the-art facility with industry-leading EBITDA margins (32%)
- Expansion into high-value automotive and advanced high-strength steel markets
- Phase II-A expansion completed November 2020 (ahead of schedule, under budget)
- Diversification of production capabilities combining traditional blast furnace with innovative EAF technology

- **Payment:** $400 million from available liquidity

## Key Observations for Nippon-U.S. Steel Analysis

1. **Regulatory Approval Speed:** Major steel M&A transactions (>$1B) typically received HSR early termination within 2-2.5 months during 2018-2020, indicating limited antitrust concerns in the sector at that time.

2. **Valuation Multiples:** Strategic acquisitions in the steel sector traded at 5.6x-6.0x EBITDA multiples during the 2018-2020 period.

3. **Deal Structure Trends:**
   - Larger strategic combinations used stock consideration to preserve cash (CLF-AK Steel)
   - Mixed consideration balanced seller preferences and buyer liquidity (CLF-ArcelorMittal)
   - All-cash deals for high-conviction strategic assets (USS-Big River, SDI transactions)

4. **Strategic Themes:**
   - Vertical integration (raw materials to finished steel)
   - Technology acquisition (EAF capabilities, advanced manufacturing)
   - Scale and market share consolidation
   - Product portfolio diversification (commodity to specialty/value-added)

5. **No Regulatory Challenges:** None of the identified transactions faced significant regulatory opposition, divestitures, or behavioral remedies during this period.
